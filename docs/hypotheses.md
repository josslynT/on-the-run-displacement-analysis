# Hypotheses & Findings — On the Run
## Part 1 — Drivers of displacement (complete)
H1. Most forcibly displaced people are internally displaced, not refugees. Most displaced people don't cross a border — they're trapped inside their own country. Finding: Supported — in 2025, ~68% of forcibly displaced people are IDPs.

H2. When people do flee across borders, the burden falls on neighbouring countries, not wealthy Western states. Finding: Supported — in 2025, Iran, Pakistan, Türkiye and Uganda alone host ~35% of the world's refugees. Refugees overwhelmingly flee to neighbours (Syria → Türkiye, Afghanistan/Iraq → Iran, Afghanistan → Pakistan, South Sudan/DR Congo → Uganda).

H3. Different crises produce different displacement. Natural disasters mainly cause internal displacement, while wars and violence drive people across borders. Finding: Supported, illustrated by three country deep-dives:

- Pakistan — disaster-dominant crises (2005 Kashmir earthquake, 2008 floods).
- Afghanistan (and Syria) — prolonged armed conflict, compounded by disaster.
- Ukraine — sudden escalation of armed conflict (2014, 2022).

## Part 2 — Predicting displacement (in progress)
H4. Poorer countries produce more displaced people for the same level of conflict or disaster (join GDP per capita / poverty indicators).

H5. Refugee outflows can be modelled from conflict intensity (fatalities, lagged one year), disaster impact, and population.

H6. Countries cluster into displacement profiles (IDP-heavy vs refugee-heavy, conflict-driven vs disaster-driven).
